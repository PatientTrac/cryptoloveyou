What Counts as a Taxable Crypto Event in 2026?

✅ Taxable Events (Report These)

  • → Selling crypto for fiat (USD, EUR, GBP)
  • → Trading one crypto for another (BTC → ETH)
  • → Using crypto to buy goods or services
  • → Receiving staking rewards (income at receipt)
  • → Receiving airdrop tokens with value
  • → Mining rewards (ordinary income)
  • → Receiving payment in crypto for work
  • → DeFi liquidity mining rewards
  • → NFT sales (capital gains)
  • → Hard fork tokens received

🚫 Generally Not Taxable

  • → Buying crypto with fiat (not a gain yet)
  • → Moving crypto between your own wallets
  • → Gifting crypto (may have gift tax rules)
  • → Donating crypto to registered charities
  • → HODLing (holding without selling)
  • → Buying crypto in a tax-advantaged account (where available)
  • ⚠️ Note: Wrapped tokens (ETH → wETH) may be taxable in some jurisdictions — check local rules

3

Choose Your Cost Basis Method (FIFO, LIFO or HIFO)

The method you use to calculate which crypto you "sold" has a massive impact on your tax bill. Each method is legal but gives different results.

FIFO (First In, First Out)
Oldest coins sold first. Required in some countries. Can create larger gains in a bull market.
LIFO (Last In, First Out)
Newest coins sold first. Can reduce gains if you bought recently at higher prices.
HIFO (Highest In, First Out)
Highest cost basis first. Often produces the lowest taxable gain. Best strategy in most cases.
4

Report Staking, Airdrops, and AI Token Rewards Separately

These are ordinary income, not capital gains. Report them at the fair market value in your local currency on the day you received them. Then when you eventually sell, the gain/loss is calculated from that initial income value.

Example: You receive 10 TAO staking rewards on March 1, 2026 when TAO = $400. You report $4,000 as ordinary income in 2026. You sell those 10 TAO in 2027 at $600 each ($6,000). You then report $2,000 in capital gains for 2027.
5

File the Right Forms for Your Country

Different countries use different forms. Make sure you're filing the right ones — missing a form is often treated as non-disclosure.

🇺🇸 USA: Form 8949 (each trade), Schedule D (totals), Schedule 1 (staking income). FBAR if offshore holdings exceed $10,000.
🇬🇧 UK: Self Assessment SA100 with supplementary pages SA108 (capital gains). HMRC pools same-day and 30-day bed-and-breakfast rules apply.
🇩🇪 Germany: Anlage SO for capital gains. Crypto held 1+ year is tax-free. Staking may reset the 1-year clock.
🇦🇺 Australia: ATO Capital Gains Tax schedule. 50% CGT discount if held 12+ months. Personal use asset exemption for small amounts.
6

Keep Records for 7+ Years

The IRS can audit up to 6 years back (or indefinitely for fraud). Store your exchange CSVs, wallet transaction exports, screenshots of prices, and tax software reports in secure cloud storage. Keep records for every wallet address — even empty ones.

🇭🇰 Hong Kong

No capital gains tax in Hong Kong. Crypto profits from trading are generally treated as personal investment returns (not taxable). However, if you trade as a business or are deemed a professional trader, profits may be subject to Profits Tax (8.25%–16.5%). The SFC regulates licensed crypto exchanges but personal crypto activities remain lightly taxed — one reason HK remains a crypto hub. Note: Consult a Hong Kong tax advisor as the distinction between investment and trading activity is case-specific.

Tax Loss Harvesting: Reduce Your Bill Legally

Tax loss harvesting is the practice of selling losing positions to offset gains, reducing your total taxable income. Unlike US stocks, there is no wash-sale rule for crypto in the US (as of 2026) — meaning you can sell, claim the loss, and immediately rebuy the same token.

How It Works

1. You made $50,000 gain from selling BTC

2. You have ETH at a $30,000 unrealised loss

3. You sell the ETH → realise the $30,000 loss

4. You immediately rebuy ETH at the same price

5. Net taxable gain: $20,000 (saving ~$7,000+ in taxes)

Important Caveats

  • ⚠️ UK has a 30-day bed-and-breakfast rule — you can't claim the loss if you rebuy within 30 days
  • ⚠️ Australia has similar "wash sale" provisions that may apply
  • ⚠️ US wash-sale rules for crypto are actively being legislated — may change

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I owe taxes if I just bought crypto and haven't sold? ▼

No. Simply buying and holding crypto is not a taxable event in any major jurisdiction. You only owe tax when you dispose of it (sell, trade, spend, or gift it) or earn it (staking, mining, airdrops). Unrealised gains are not taxed.

Is sending crypto between my own wallets taxable? ▼

Generally no, if you own both wallets. Transfers between your own wallets (Coinbase → your Ledger wallet, for example) are not taxable events. However, you need to track these transfers so your tax software doesn't incorrectly flag them as sales. Gas fees paid on transfers may be deductible in some jurisdictions.

What if I traded crypto years ago and didn't report it? ▼

You have options. The IRS Voluntary Disclosure Program (VDP) allows you to come forward proactively and typically results in lower penalties than if the IRS discovers the non-disclosure first. HMRC has a similar Digital Disclosure Service. Given that exchanges now report your data automatically, getting compliant now is strongly advisable. Consult a crypto tax attorney or CPA immediately.

Are NFT sales taxed differently to regular crypto? ▼

In the US, the IRS treats NFTs as capital assets — same rules as crypto (Form 8949, short/long-term gains). However, some high-value NFTs may be classified as "collectibles" which have a higher 28% long-term CGT rate rather than the standard 20%. Royalties received from NFT sales are ordinary income. The UK and Australia treat NFTs similarly to other crypto assets.

How are DeFi lending and borrowing taxed? ▼

This is genuinely complex and varies by jurisdiction. Generally: depositing crypto as collateral to borrow stablecoins is NOT a taxable event (you haven't sold). Interest received from lending crypto may be ordinary income. Interest paid to borrow may be deductible if used for investment purposes. However, some tax authorities treat certain DeFi wrapping/depositing transactions as disposals. Use tax software and consult a professional for significant DeFi activity.

Can I deduct crypto trading losses? ▼

Yes. Realised losses offset gains dollar-for-dollar. If your losses exceed your gains, in the US you can deduct up to $3,000 of net capital losses against ordinary income per year, and carry forward the rest indefinitely. The UK has a similar system with annual allowances. This is why tax-loss harvesting is such an effective strategy.
