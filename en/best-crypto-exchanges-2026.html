Detailed Exchange Reviews 2026

1. Coinbase — Best for Beginners

★★★★★ Security · ★★★ Fees · ★★★★ Token Selection

Visit →

Coinbase is the gold standard for security and regulatory compliance. As a NASDAQ-listed public company (COIN), it faces the same disclosure requirements as traditional financial institutions — giving users a level of transparency no other crypto exchange can match. Its US Money Transmitter Licenses, UK FCA registration, and EU MiCA compliance mean it operates legally in the most regulated markets.

The trade-off is fees. Coinbase charges 0.5–1.5% for simple buys, significantly higher than Kraken or Binance. However, using Coinbase Advanced Trade (accessible from the same account) reduces this to 0.4% maker / 0.6% taker. For larger amounts, always use Advanced Trade.

Pros

  • ✓ NASDAQ-listed, highest regulatory standing
  • ✓ FDIC insurance on USD balances (up to $250k)
  • ✓ Best beginner interface in the industry
  • ✓ Advanced Trade for lower fees
  • ✓ Coinbase One subscription for zero trading fees

Cons

  • ✗ Highest fees of any top exchange
  • ✗ Fewer altcoins than Binance or Bybit
  • ✗ Customer support can be slow
  • ✗ Locked staking (limited countries)

2. Kraken — Best Security Record

★★★★★ Security · ★★★★ Fees · ★★★★ Token Selection

Visit →

Founded in 2011, Kraken has operated for 15 years without a major security breach — a remarkable record given that virtually every other top exchange has been hacked at some point. This is partly due to Kraken's conservative approach: they publish regular Proof of Reserves attestations, hold the majority of assets in cold storage, and have a reputation for prioritising security over growth speed.

Kraken Pro offers competitive maker fees of 0.16% and taker of 0.26%, significantly cheaper than Coinbase. The interface is slightly more complex but perfectly manageable for beginners using the basic Kraken (non-Pro) interface. Staking rewards of 4–17% APY on selected assets are available in supported jurisdictions.

Pros

  • ✓ Zero major hacks in 15-year history
  • ✓ Strong Proof of Reserves programme
  • ✓ Regulated in US, EU, UK, Canada, Australia
  • ✓ Kraken Pro for low fees
  • ✓ Strong staking product

Cons

  • ✗ Interface less polished than Coinbase
  • ✗ Fewer altcoins than Binance
  • ✗ Some AI tokens not listed
  • ✗ Fiat on-ramp can be slower

3. Binance — Best Fees & Token Selection

★★★★ Security · ★★★★★ Fees · ★★★★★ Token Selection

Visit →

Binance is the world's largest crypto exchange by trading volume and offers the most comprehensive token selection, including nearly all AI crypto projects: TAO, RENDER, ASI, NEAR, ICP, IO, and GRASS. With a standard trading fee of 0.1% (reduced to 0.075% when paying fees in BNB), Binance is dramatically cheaper than Coinbase.

The elephant in the room is regulation. Binance has faced regulatory actions from the CFTC, DOJ, and multiple national regulators. CEO Changpeng Zhao paid a $50M personal fine in 2024. That said, Binance continues to operate legally in 180+ countries and has significantly improved its compliance. It is not available to US residents — use Binance.US if American.

Pros

  • ✓ Earn products (staking, savings)
  • ✓ Built-in P2P marketplace

  • ✗ Customer support slow at peak times

Which Exchange Should You Use by Country?

🇺🇸 USA

Coinbase (safest, regulated), Kraken (lower fees, also regulated), Binance.US (separate entity, limited token selection). Avoid the international Binance.com — illegal for US residents.

🇬🇧 UK

Coinbase (FCA registered), Kraken (FCA registered), Binance (available with restrictions). UK users cannot access leveraged products on most exchanges due to FCA rules.

🇪🇺 Europe

Binance (MiCA compliant), Kraken (strong EU presence), Coinbase (available). Germany users should note staking rules — some products restricted by BaFin.

🇦🇺 Australia

Coinbase (AUSTRAC registered), Kraken, Binance AU (separate, AUSTRAC registered). Independent Australian Exchange Independent Reserve also worth considering.

🇭🇰 Hong Kong

Bybit (SFC licensed), OKX, Binance. Hong Kong has created a clear licensing framework — several exchanges are now SFC-licensed and operating legally.

🌏 Southeast Asia

Bybit (popular in SG, TH, PH), Binance, OKX. Check local regulations — Singapore (MAS), Thailand (SEC), Philippines (BSP) all have crypto frameworks.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to keep crypto on an exchange?

For small amounts you plan to trade actively, keeping on a regulated exchange (Coinbase, Kraken) is reasonably safe. For larger amounts or long-term holding, always move to a self-custody hardware wallet. Exchanges can be hacked, frozen, or go bankrupt — as FTX demonstrated in 2022.

Do exchanges report to tax authorities?

Yes. Since 2024, US exchanges must send 1099-DA forms to the IRS. EU exchanges report under DAC8. UK exchanges report to HMRC. Australia exchanges report to the ATO. There is no legal way to hide crypto trading from tax authorities if you used a KYC-verified exchange.

Can I use multiple exchanges?

Yes, and many experienced traders do. A common approach is to use Coinbase or Kraken for buying with fiat (regulated, beginner-friendly) and Binance or Bybit for trading AI tokens and altcoins (better selection, lower fees). Just make sure to track all transactions across all exchanges for tax purposes.

What is the difference between a DEX and a CEX?

A CEX (centralised exchange like Coinbase or Binance) is a company that holds your funds and matches buyers/sellers. A DEX (decentralised exchange like Uniswap) is a smart contract on blockchain — no company involved, you trade directly from your own wallet. DEXs require more technical knowledge but give you full control of your assets.
