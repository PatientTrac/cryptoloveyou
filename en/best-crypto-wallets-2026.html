Cold wallets

Hardware wallets like Ledger and Trezor store your keys completely offline. The gold standard for security — essential for holdings over $500.

📱

Hot wallets

Software wallets like Trust Wallet and Exodus are free, convenient, and good for active trading. Less secure than hardware — never store large sums.

🔑

Seed phrases

Your 12–24 word recovery phrase is your crypto. Write it on paper, store it offline, never photograph it or type it online. Losing it = losing everything.

🤖

AI coin support

Not all wallets support newer AI tokens. Check compatibility for TAO (Bittensor), RNDR (Render), FET (Fetch.ai), and other EVM or Substrate-based tokens.

Frequently Asked Questions

Hardware wallets (cold wallets) are the safest option. Ledger and Trezor are the two most trusted brands in 2026. They keep your private keys offline, making them immune to online hacks. For small amounts used for daily trading, a reputable software wallet like MetaMask or Trust Wallet is acceptable.

A hot wallet is connected to the internet — apps like MetaMask, Trust Wallet, or Phantom. Convenient for frequent use but more vulnerable to hacks. A cold wallet stores your private keys offline on a physical device. It is the most secure option for long-term storage of significant holdings.

If you have your 12 or 24-word seed phrase safely backed up, you can recover all funds by importing it into any compatible wallet. If you lose both the device and the seed phrase, your crypto is permanently lost. Always back up your seed phrase on paper in a fireproof location — never digitally.

A seed phrase is 12 or 24 random words that act as the master key to your wallet. Anyone who has it can access all your funds. Protect it by writing it on paper, storing it in a fireproof safe, never photographing it, and never sharing it — even with supposed support staff.

MetaMask remains the most widely used Ethereum wallet and is generally safe when used correctly. Main risks are phishing sites, malicious browser extensions, and accidentally signing scam transactions. Always download from the official site, never share your seed phrase, and use a hardware wallet for large amounts.
